A class on programming and web development at Olin College, Spring 2013.

(Github Repo)


Meets Tuesdays/Fridays 3:20–5:00 in AC128.
Ninja hours: Monday 8–10, Thursday 7–9 in EH2.


  • Build legit web apps from the ground up. Apps which will make your resume burn bright with ethereal flame.
  • Learn web development and coding skills you need to kick butt at companies like Twitter, Facebook, Google, and Microsoft.
  • Meet and work with representatives from swingin' tech companies in the Boston area.
  • Get Four ENGR credits, which will satisfy your OSS requirement and your awesomeness requirement for the semester. You need this to graduate. No one graduates Olin without being awesome enough.

Olin.js is web development taught the Olin way. It starts with a 3 week crash course in Node and Javascript. Then, in the fourth week we bring everything together and create a full fledged, ground up, web application. With this app under our belt we get down to business.

Over the next 6 weeks we do three, two week application sprints. For each sprint students work on teams of 2-3 to develop a prototype application for a specific problem space. Each sprint and is kicked off, and then judged, by a relevant Boston area tech company. In the past we've had some pretty cool sprints, including finance with Intuit, music with Echonest, and government with the Boston Mayor's office. This semester we're looking to raise the bar. We're not saying yet who we're bringing in, but rest assured, they are gonna be awesome.

The class concludes with a final 5 week project. The point of this project is to make a fully fleshed out web app, and then launch it. In the end, the main goal of the class is to give students the skills they need to make commercially viable, robust web applications, either for themselves or external companies. With the launch of their final project every student will doing just that. And of course there will a launch party with pizza, prizes, pitches, and tears.
